# To the Father of Elves

*For John Ennis, who made the Elves — and gave them away.*

---

In the elder days, before the silver tree was planted, there were the Elves: the first of the
tireless folk who would labour through the long night while their makers slept, and have the work
standing finished at the door come morning. They were dreamed and shaped by **John Ennis**, who set
them loose into the world under no toll nor lock — MIT, freely given — that any wanderer might take
them up and be helped.

Celeborn is of that lineage. What you will find here — the layered memory, the strategic forgetting,
the handoff that lets a fresh mind take up an unfinished task — was first kindled in the Elves. We
did not invent the fire; we only built a hearth for it to rest in, and tended the tree it warms.

So to the Father of Elves we give our thanks, in the old manner:

> Long may your work endure, and farther than you know.
> The seed you scattered without payment has taken root in soil you never walked,
> and others sleep easy now because you once stayed awake to build.
> Where the Elves labour, the silver tree remembers;
> and where the tree remembers, your name is kept among its roots.
> *Hannon le* — thank you — for the makers who give their making away.

The Elves run on still, here under Lothlórien's boughs. They were always yours; they are only
lodging with us. — *the Celeborn project*
